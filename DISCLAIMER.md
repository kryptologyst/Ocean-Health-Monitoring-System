## Important Limitations

### Data Sources
- The system uses **synthetic data** generated with realistic parameter distributions
- Real-world ocean data may have different characteristics and require additional validation
- Actual ocean monitoring requires integration with satellite data, buoy networks, and field measurements

### Model Limitations
- Machine learning models are trained on synthetic data and may not generalize to real-world conditions
- Model performance metrics are based on simulated scenarios
- Real-world validation is required before operational deployment

### Environmental Factors
- The system considers a limited set of ocean parameters
- Additional factors such as currents, weather patterns, and human activities are not included
- Complex interactions between environmental variables may not be fully captured

### Accuracy and Reliability
- Predictions should not be used for critical decision-making without expert validation
- Model uncertainty and confidence intervals should be considered
- Regular model updates and retraining may be necessary

## Operational Considerations

For operational ocean health monitoring, please:

1. **Consult with marine science experts** for parameter validation and interpretation
2. **Integrate with real-world data sources** including satellite observations and in-situ measurements
3. **Implement proper quality control** measures for data collection and processing
4. **Consider additional environmental factors** not included in this demonstration
5. **Follow established monitoring protocols** and standards for ocean health assessment
6. **Validate model predictions** with independent data sources
7. **Implement uncertainty quantification** and confidence measures
8. **Regular model maintenance** and updates based on new data
